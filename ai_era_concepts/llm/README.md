# LLM: The Foundation

## Overview
Large Language Models (LLMs) are deep learning models trained on vast amounts of text data to understand, generate, and reason with human language.

## Why it matters in the AI Era
LLMs have shifted software development from "deterministic logic" to "probabilistic reasoning." Instead of writing every rule, developers can now instruct models using natural language.

---

## Key Principles
1. **Transformer Architecture:** The "Attention" mechanism that allows models to focus on relevant parts of the input sequence.
2. **Pre-training:** Learning general patterns from the internet.
3. **Fine-tuning / Alignment:** Adapting the model for specific tasks or safety guidelines.

---

## AI Context
LLMs are the "engine" behind AI agents, chatbots, and copilot tools. Understanding their limits (e.g., training cut-off, reasoning vs. retrieval) is crucial for building robust systems.
